## Patterns observed

**1. The extractor over-indexes on the verb "supports" wherever Lindsay agrees with anything in the room.** Roughly half the entries are some variant of "Lindsay supports X" where X is a feature, a project, a specific staffing choice, or a date. Agreement-in-meeting is not the same as a held position. The extractor treats every approval like a stance.

**2. The "concerned" direction is heavily used for observations.** When Lindsay notes a problem or flags risk, the extractor labels it a stance. But "concerned that X is happening" is most often an observation about the present, not a transferable position. The 84 observation-mode SKIPs largely come from this pattern.

**3. Long, dense meeting transcripts produce the most noise.** The 2026-04-14-claude-code-for-reserv-product transcript yielded ~20 stances of which maybe 4 are real. Same for 2026-03-23-product-managers-internal-bi-weekly (10 entries, 1-2 real). Long working-session meetings where Lindsay reacts to many discrete items generate many low-quality stances. Conversely, the 1-on-1 transcripts (john-lindsay-11) produce a higher rate of real stances because Lindsay is articulating positions, not reacting to slides.

**4. The extractor never deduplicates across sessions.** Lindsay says some variant of "Snapsheet tasks are the wrong model" / "get adjusters out of Snapsheet" / "POP off Snapsheet" in approximately 15 different meetings. Each becomes its own stance entry. The same underlying conviction gets stamped over and over with slightly different wording. A real stance extractor needs cross-session deduplication.

**5. Action-items and stances are systematically conflated.** "Lindsay wants X built", "Lindsay plans to schedule Y", "Lindsay supports prioritizing Z" — all become stances. These belong in commitments. The extractor has no concept of intent-to-act vs. position-held.

**6. Direction labels (`supports`/`opposes`/`concerned`/`neutral`) are applied loosely.** "Neutral" often means "Lindsay said something factual" — not a stance at all. "Supports" is the default whenever the sentence is positive in tone. The direction field is overloaded and isn't doing useful work.

## Recommended prompt criteria for rewrite

Based on the failure modes above, these specific rules would dramatically improve precision:

1. **Reject intent-to-act verbs.** If the predicate is "wants X built", "plans to do Y", "will schedule Z", "advocates for hiring W", "proposed that we do V" — that is a commitment or proposal, not a stance. Reroute these to the commitments stream and do not extract as a stance.

2. **Reject present-tense observations.** Phrases like "engineers are slipping", "the data appeared incomplete", "the team has not yet decided" describe a state of the world. They are not positions held by the person. Stances must be statements *about how things should be*, not statements *about how things currently are*.

3. **Reject single-meeting framings.** If the subject of the stance is a specific named project, a specific meeting, a specific deadline, or a specific colleague's situation — and would be incomprehensible without that context — it is not a stance. Stances generalize.

4. **Require a contestability test.** Before extracting, ask: "Could a reasonable colleague hold the opposite view?" If the position is "we should ship the thing" or "this is important" or "we need to maintain velocity" — anyone would agree. Drop it. Only extract positions where there's a real alternative.

5. **Require a transfer test.** Before extracting, ask: "Would the person still articulate this position in an unrelated conversation 3 months later?" If the position only makes sense within a specific sprint, project, or meeting, drop it.

6. **Drop weak-position verbs.** "Acknowledged", "clarified", "agreed", "confirmed", "noted" — these signal Lindsay was responding to someone else's framing, not asserting her own position. Only extract when she's articulating *her own* view forcefully.

7. **Cross-session deduplication.** Before saving a new stance, search existing stances for the same underlying position expressed differently. Merge into the existing entry rather than creating a duplicate. If "Replicating tasks in Glance — opposes" already exists, don't extract a near-identical entry from the next meeting.

8. **Cap extraction per meeting.** A single meeting should produce 0-3 stances at most. If a single meeting yields 10+ stances, the extractor is treating the meeting like a list of agenda items. Tighten until precision wins over recall.

9. **Drop neutral-direction entries entirely.** If the extractor labels something "neutral", it's not a stance. Either the person took a position or they didn't.

10. **Require a "this is contestable / persistent / distinctive" sentence in the extractor's chain-of-thought.** Make the LLM explicitly justify each extraction. Discarded extractions teach the model what to skip.

Expected outcome with these rules: 50-75 stances per person at the target precision, with stance count growing slowly over time as new genuine positions emerge — not the current pattern of stance count growing linearly with meeting count.
