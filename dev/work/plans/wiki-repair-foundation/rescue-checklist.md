## What "keep" actually costs (for calibration)
- **Money/perf: zero.** Frozen pages are no-ops in refresh walks; no LLM spend; disk trivial.
- **Lint nag: capped.** One stale topic per winddown, regardless of how many are stale.
- **Retrieval: labeled, minor.** Stale pages can appear in search/briefs but now carry
  "(as of 2026-04-24 — stale)" labels; worst case they occupy a ranking slot a live page wanted.
- **Dormancy is graceful now:** past 90d they fall off the extraction bias list (hibernate); if a
  topic revives, mentions surface as orphan tags via the chef's nudge → restore/refresh is trivial.
Archive is curation hygiene, not resource savings. Bias to keep is sound.
